Bouldering versus other types of climbing

At the Outdoor Adventures space on campus, there is a bouldering wall available free to students, and to faculty for a small fee.

Bouldering differes from other kinds of climbing in that there is no harness around your body and there are no ropes to catch you if you fall. Because of this, bouldering is typically done, well, on boulders, big rocks. Bouldering can be done both inside and outside. Outside bouldering tries to mitigate possible harm by having a safety mat under you while you climb. This mat has some foam cushion and is a only a few inches thick as a lot of outdoor boulding spots require a short hike through the wilderness. You don't want to be luggin around a 30 pound mat of foam to and from your climbing site.

On campus we only have indoor bouldering. This consists of a tall and very wide wall of 14 ft. and 60 ft. respectively. Indoor bouldering differs from outdoor in that the foam mat to catch you is much thicker, cushioning your fall more, and the problems on the wall can be changed any day of the week. The student employees of the climbing wall have specific people called "setters". They create the different problems on the wall. The routes are changed on a bi-weekly basis. Each bi-week, only two colors are changed.

Climbing Difficulties and Grades

Each climbing gym has its own color code to signify the difficulty of each route. The climbing wall on campus uses the following:

  1. Beginner - Green
  2. Easy - Yellow
  3. Lower Intermediate - Orange
  4. Upper Intermediate - Blue
  5. Advanced - Black
  6. Expert - White

Though the colors define a range of diffculty, there are ranges of diffculty for each color. This range is from VB to V17 or even more depending on who you talk to, VB stands for V-Beginner. This V-Scale is what is used for outdoor bouldering, as there are no different colored holds to signify difficulty.
